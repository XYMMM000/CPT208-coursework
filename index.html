About This Portfolio

This portfolio is designed to present not only the final outcome of my project, but also the thinking, planning, and development process behind it. It records how ideas were formed, how decisions were made, what challenges appeared, and how the project evolved over time.

By documenting the process in detail, I hope to show both the technical work and the reflective learning that took place during the project.

Project Overview

This project began with an initial idea and gradually developed through research, experimentation, and iteration. Rather than focusing only on the final result, this website highlights the entire journey from concept to completion.

It includes background research, design decisions, prototype development, implementation steps, and reflections on what was successful and what could be improved in future work.

Key Stages

Research

I investigated relevant background information, examples, and technical possibilities in order to build a solid foundation for the project.

Design

I explored different concepts, structures, and visual ideas before deciding on a direction that best matched the goals of the project.

Implementation

I developed the project step by step, tested its core features, and refined the final outcome through repeated adjustments and improvements.

Project Process

Step 1 — Initial Idea

At the beginning, I identified the central purpose of the project and clarified what I wanted to create, who it was for, and why it would be meaningful.

Step 2 — Background Research

I reviewed related work, reference materials, and existing examples to better understand possible approaches and to define the scope of the project.

Step 3 — Early Design Exploration

I sketched ideas, compared alternatives, and considered different structures before choosing a design direction that balanced creativity and practicality.

Step 4 — Development

I implemented the main features of the project and gradually improved them through testing, debugging, and iterative refinement.

Step 5 — Review and Improvement

After testing the project, I identified areas that could be improved and made revisions to strengthen the final outcome and overall presentation.

Reflection

This project helped me better understand the importance of process, not just outcome. Recording each stage encouraged me to think more carefully about my decisions, evaluate my progress, and reflect on how the work changed over time.

Looking back, the most valuable part of the project was not only building the final result, but also learning through the cycles of research, design, testing, and revision.
