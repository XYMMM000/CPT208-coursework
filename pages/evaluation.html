Evaluation

Usability Testing and Reflection

Testing Plan

We conducted usability testing with three target users to evaluate whether the prototype was clear, motivating, and suitable for use in an onsite climbing environment. Participants were asked to complete key tasks such as selecting a route challenge, checking progress, and reading coaching feedback.

Participant 1

Beginner climber with limited prior climbing experience.

Participant 2

Intermediate climber familiar with climbing gyms and route grading.

Participant 3

Experienced climber / mentor interested in coaching and feedback tools.

Key Findings

  • Users liked the route challenge framing because it made sessions feel more structured.
  • The badge and progress elements increased motivation and made improvement easier to notice.
  • Some users wanted more visual clarity in navigation and larger buttons for mobile interaction.
  • Feedback cards were seen as useful, but users preferred concise wording and clear tags.

Usability Testing Results: Detailed Findings

Aspect Participant Feedback Action Taken
Route Selection Clear recommendations; one user wanted more context about why a route was suggested Added brief reason (e.g., "Build on climbing skills")
Progress Display Badges were motivating; streak counter was memorable Emphasized streak counter and badge visuals
Coaching Feedback Users appreciated quick notes, but wanted emoji or icons for fast scanning Added emoji tags to feedback cards (e.g., 💪, 🎯)
Mobile Usability Buttons and text were hard to tap on small screens Increased touch target size; improved line-height

Iterative Refinement: Before and After

Before: The initial prototype had dense text on route selection, small buttons, and unclear navigation.

After: We streamlined the layout, increased whitespace, added visual icons, and made buttons larger. Feedback cards now use emoji and shorter text, and progress is displayed more prominently.

Note: Before and after screenshots will be uploaded in the final submission.

Final Reflection: Design Implications

Human-Centred Design Outcome: This project demonstrates the importance of grounding design decisions in user research. By engaging directly with climbers and coaches, we were able to identify authentic pain points that generic fitness apps overlook. The iterative evaluation process confirmed that even small tweaks (emoji, spacing, wording) significantly impact user confidence and motivation.

Social and Ethical Considerations: We intentionally avoided competitive leaderboards or judgment-based framing. Instead, ClimbQuest emphasises personal growth, peer support, and lightweight coaching. This design choice reflects our commitment to accessibility and inclusion, ensuring that climbers of all levels feel welcomed and supported, not intimidated.

AI Reflection: During this project, we used AI tools for code scaffolding and design ideation (as permitted). However, all user research, persona development, core design logic, and evaluation decisions were conducted autonomously by the team. AI was used as a productivity assistant, not as a substitute for authentic human-centred design thinking. All AI-generated code was carefully reviewed and modified to align with our specific user requirements and testing feedback.

Social and Ethical Reflection

We considered issues of accessibility, user confidence, and inclusion. The system avoids overly competitive framing and instead supports gradual growth, encouragement, and low-pressure participation for beginners.

Technical Reflection

During development, we focused on keeping the prototype lightweight and mobile-friendly. We also reflected on the role of AI-assisted tools in ideation and implementation, ensuring that key design decisions remained grounded in our own research and testing.
