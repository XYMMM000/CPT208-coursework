User Requirements

Personas, Journey, and Needs

Primary Persona: Amy

Age: 21

Profile: Beginner indoor climber

Goals: Find suitable routes, feel less intimidated, and understand how to improve.

Frustrations: Confusing route choices, lack of confidence, and difficulty knowing what to do next after failure.

Secondary Persona: Jason

Age: 28

Profile: Part-time coach / experienced climber

Goals: Give fast, helpful advice and keep beginners motivated without disrupting gym flow.

Frustrations: No lightweight tool to organise route recommendations and coaching feedback onsite.

User Journey Map

Before climbing: User arrives at gym unsure which route to try.

During climbing: User attempts routes, fails, retries, and needs feedback and encouragement.

After climbing: User wants to reflect, record progress, and decide what to improve next time.

Main Pain Points

  • Difficulty selecting an appropriate route level
  • Lack of confidence after repeated failed attempts
  • No structured sense of progress during a session
  • Feedback from coaches is inconsistent or hard to remember
  • Motivation drops without playful or social reinforcement

Design Opportunities

  • Create route recommendations based on user level
  • Provide playful milestones and visual progress
  • Support quick coaching feedback cards
  • Encourage repeat use through badges and session achievements
  • Keep the system simple and mobile-friendly for onsite use

Must-Have Features

Three Core Playful Requirements

Route Quest

A personalised route challenge system that recommends suitable climbing tasks based on the user’s level and confidence.

Progress Badges

A playful reward layer that visualises achievements, improvement, and climbing consistency across sessions.

Coach Feedback Cards

Short feedback notes from coaches or experienced climbers to support reflection and learning.
