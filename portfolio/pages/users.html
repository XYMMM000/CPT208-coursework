User Requirements

User Personas

Featured Persona

“If I can clearly see the next route challenge, I feel motivated to come back every week.”

Age 22 University Student Indoor Climbing Beginner

Name: Rin Zhao

Background: Rin climbs 1-2 times per week and mostly uses mobile tools inside the gym to track progress.

Goals

  • Choose routes that match current level without hesitation
  • See visible progress after each session
  • Build confidence through clear milestones

Pain Points

  • Route difficulty labels feel inconsistent for beginners
  • After failure, next steps are not obvious
  • Feedback is fragmented and hard to remember

Design Opportunity

Provide a playful “next best route” recommendation with confidence cues, mini-achievements, and coach-ready feedback cards in one view.

Edge Persona

“I only climb occasionally, so I need very clear guidance and fast feedback every single time.”

Age 27 Working Professional Low-frequency Visitor

Name: Leo Sun

Background: Leo visits the gym once or twice a month and often feels unfamiliar with route choices after a long break.

Goals

  • Get a quick route suggestion based on current condition
  • Avoid spending time guessing what to try first
  • Leave each session with at least one small achievement

Pain Points

  • Forgets previous progress between sessions
  • Feels lost when route labels are too technical
  • Needs lightweight guidance without complex setup

Design Opportunity

Add a “returning climber mode” that restores past progress, suggests one starter route, and gives a one-screen action plan.

User Journey

Current Climbing Experience

User Journey Map

The following journey map illustrates the typical climbing session workflow and identifies key pain points where our system can intervene:

Stage Action Current Pain Point
1. Arrival Climber enters gym and checks in No context about today's session objective
2. Route Selection Choose which route to attempt first Routes feel arbitrary; unclear progression
3. Climbing Attempt Climber attempts route and may succeed or fail No structured feedback during attempt
4. Post-Attempt Rest and reflect on performance Unclear what to try next or how to improve
5. Progress Tracking End of session recap Hard to see progress or celebrate wins

User Requirements: Must-Haves for "Playful"

  1. Guided Route Selection: The system must recommend routes based on user level and past performance, transforming selection from random guessing into a structured, guided journey that feels supportive rather than judgmental.
  2. Visible Progress and Achievement: Users must see immediate, tangible progress through badges, challenge milestones, or streak counters, so they feel motivated to return and can easily celebrate small wins during or after each session.
  3. Coach-Ready Feedback Integration: The system must enable coaches to quickly provide lightweight, contextual feedback (e.g., "nice footwork on that move") linked to specific attempts, making users feel seen, supported, and more confident in future attempts.

Evidence of Life: User Observations

We conducted informal observations and interviews with climbers at the gym to validate our assumptions. Key findings confirmed that:

  • Beginner climbers spend 5-10 minutes choosing routes without a clear strategy
  • Regular climbers rely on memory or coach suggestions to know what to try next
  • Climbers rarely remember past attempts or see tangible progress over weeks
  • Coaching feedback is often verbal and quickly forgotten after the session
  • Many climbers climb solo and lack motivation or accountability
